Why join BVIYHTA?

Marine Tourism — Yachting, Charters, Ferries, Dive Shops & Water Taxis

The BVI is built on the water.
Every marine business deserves a voice in how it's governed.

"From the bareboat that left Nanny Cay this morning, to the water taxi crossing to Jost Van Dyke, to the dive boat heading to the Rhone — marine tourism is the heartbeat of the BVI. BVIYHTA is here to make sure that heartbeat is heard."

The BVI's marine tourism sector is extraordinarily diverse. Bareboat and crewed charter companies. Water taxis and inter-island ferries. Dive operators and snorkel tour guides. Day-trip boats and fishing charters. Kayak rentals. Parasail operators. Boatyards, fuel docks, chandleries, and marine engineers. Each is a distinct business — but every one of them shares the same waters, the same regulatory environment, and the same need to be represented when decisions are made.

BVIYHTA works alongside — not instead of — existing marine industry bodies like the BVI Marine Association. Our role is complementary: BVIYHTA brings the marine sector's concerns into the broader tourism and hospitality conversation, engaging with Government, the BVI Tourist Board, and destination management partners on the cross-cutting issues that affect everyone who depends on tourism. Where specialist bodies have deep domain expertise, BVIYHTA amplifies their voice at a higher level.

1
Broader policy reach — Marine issues don't exist in isolation. Airlift, cruise policy, destination marketing, resort development — all affect marine businesses. BVIYHTA connects the dots, ensuring marine operators are part of every relevant tourism conversation, not just marine-specific ones.
2
Ferry & water taxi advocacy — Inter-island connectivity is critical infrastructure for both residents and visitors. BVIYHTA advocates for investment in ferry terminals, water taxi access points, and scheduling policies that work for operators and tourists alike.
3
Environmental protection as a business priority — The reefs, wrecks, bays, and anchorages are your product. BVIYHTA supports policies that protect marine ecosystems — mooring ball maintenance, reef conservation, marine park management — because a degraded environment is bad for business, not just bad for nature.
4
Events, regattas & visibility — The BVI Sailing Festival, Spring Regatta, and other marine events generate enormous visitor spend across the whole economy. BVIYHTA helps secure Government and destination-level support for these events, and ensures marine businesses are central to how the BVI markets itself internationally.
5
Training & standards — From dive safety certifications to ferry crew training, access BVIYHTA's shared training and professional development resources to raise standards across the marine tourism sector — and protect the BVI's reputation as a world-class marine destination.

Whether you run a single water taxi or a fleet of bareboat charters, whether you take visitors below the surface or race them across it — the BVI's waters are your business. BVIYHTA ensures that business has a seat at every table that matters.

"The BVI Marine Association knows the water. BVIYHTA knows the room. Together, the marine sector can't be ignored."
